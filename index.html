Project Overview

Salifort Motors is facing a high employee turnover rate (~17.5%), leading to increased recruitment costs, productivity loss, and operational instability.

This project focuses on identifying key drivers of employee attrition and building a highly reliable machine learning model to proactively flag employees at risk of leaving, enabling targeted HR interventions.

Data

  • Dataset: 14,999 employee records from internal HR surveys and employment history.
  • Target variable: Attrition (left = 1, stayed = 0).

Key Features

  • Satisfaction level and last evaluation score.
  • Average monthly hours and number of projects.
  • Salary level, promotions, and tenure.
  • Department and role attributes.

Data Challenges

  • Strong class imbalance (fewer leavers than stayers).
  • High correlation between workload, satisfaction, and tenure.
  • Categorical variables requiring encoding.

EDA confirmed that satisfaction, workload, salary, and career stagnation are the strongest signals differentiating leavers from stayers.

Modeling & Statistical Validation

Random Forest was selected as the final model due to the best balance of precision, recall, and F1-score.

Tree-based models outperformed linear models due to non-linear attrition patterns.

Approach

  • Conducted detailed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand attrition patterns.
  • Encoded categorical variables and scaled numerical features.
  • Applied SMOTE to address class imbalance in employee attrition.
  • Built and compared multiple classification models:
  • Logistic Regression (baseline)
  • Decision Tree
  • Random Forest
  • XGBoost

Model Performance

  • Evaluated using Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC.
  • The final model generalizes well with no signs of overfitting and is production-ready.
  • Accuracy: 98.3%
  • Precision: 0.98
  • Recall: 0.92
  • F1-score: 0.95
  • ROC-AUC: 0.97

Interactive Power BI Analytics

Power BI dashboards were developed to translate model outputs into HR-friendly insights, enabling leadership to explore attrition risk interactively and effectively.

Insights indicate that around ~83% of employees fall into a low-risk zone, allowing for targeted retention strategies rather than blanket policies.

  • Segmented employees into risk bands to highlight high-risk concentration.

Key Insights

  • Satisfaction level is the strongest predictor of attrition.
  • Employees with satisfaction < 0.4 have 4‚Äì6√ó higher risk.
  • Low salary sharply increases turnover risk, especially for mid-tenure employees.
  • High workload (monthly hours) is a strong burnout indicator.
  • Tenure of ~3‚Äì4 years shows the highest attrition probability (career stagnation phase).
  • Department has a minor impact compared to individual experience factors.
  • Model predictions closely mirror actual attrition patterns, confirming reliability.

Business Recommendations

  • Launch employee satisfaction improvement programs.
  • Implement workload balancing and manager oversight.
  • Review salary bands for high-risk, mid-tenure employees.
  • Introduce clear promotion and career progression paths.
  • Deploy weekly attrition risk scoring dashboards.
  • Use 0.35 probability threshold for proactive HR action.
